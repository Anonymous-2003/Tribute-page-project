Pasang Lhamu Sherpa

1961 – 1993

First Nepalese Woman To Climb The Summit Of Mount Everest

Pasang Lhamu Sherpa 10 December 1961 – 22 April 1993 Pasang Lhamu Sherpa (10 December 1961 – 22 April 1993) was a courageous Nepali mountaineer who was born into a family with a rich history in climbing. From a young age, she was drawn to the mountains and began her climbing journey in her teens. Pasang Lhamu achieved numerous remarkable feats, including summiting prestigious peaks like Mount Blanc, Cho Oyu, Yalapic, Pisang Himal, and others.


Though she faced setbacks in her pursuit of the world’s highest peak, making three unsuccessful attempts at climbing Mount Everest, her determination never wavered. On April 22, 1993, she became the first Nepali woman to reach the summit of Mount Everest, ascending via the South Col and Southeast Ridge route.


Pasang Lhamu Sherpa's legacy lives on as an inspiration to mountaineers around the world. Her strength, resilience, and unwavering dedication to her dreams have earned her a place in the history of Himalayan exploration.

Facts About Pasang Lhamu Sherpa

  • She was born on December 16, 1961 in Solukhumbu, Nepal.
  • She began trekking in the mountains as a child with her father.
  • She climbed Mount Cho Oyu, Yala Peak, Pisang, and Mount Blanc.
  • She attempted to climb Mount Everest three times before reaching the summit on April 22, 1993.
  • She died during her descent from the summit due to a severe storm.
  • Her body was recovered and brought to Kathmandu after the weather improved.
